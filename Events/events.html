Day One: Friday, May 16

Opening ceremony: 11:00am - 2:00pm

Enjoy a parade of lion and dragon dancing along Grant Avenue from California to Broadway Street. Later accompanied with kung fu demonstrations, lion dance performances, and traditional chinese folk dance by troupes throughout the US on the main stage at Portsmouth square. Finishing off the opening ceremony is a closing lion dance performance with all of the troupes.

Concert: 3:00pm - 6:30pm

A concert of Hong Kong's famous singers starring Andy Lau, Aaron Kwok, Priscilla Chan, Hacken Lee, and many more to be announced

Day Two: Saturday, May 17

Legend of the Purple Hairpin（紫钗记）11:00am - 2:30pm

Kicking off day two's festival is an opening of live Cantonese opera. It is the famous play of "The Legend of the Purple Hairpin"(紫釵記) starring the famous Chor Fung Ming Cantonese Opera Troupe (雛鳳鳴劇團). The play follows the love story of poet Li Yi and a female entertainer Huo Xiaoyu around a hairpin.

In the Mood for Love （花樣年華）3:00pm - 4:45pm

Following the live stage opera comes with a film screening of "In the Mood for Love” （花樣年華）. Critically acclaimed and considered as one of the best Hong Kong films of all time. Set in 1960s Hong Kong, it follows a man Chow Mo-wan (Tony Leung) and Su Li-zhen (Maggie Cheung) who slowly fall in love after finding out their spouses are having affairs. Screening will be held at the Great Star Theater at 636 Jackson Street.

Basic conversational cantonese lesson 5:30pm - 6:30pm

Come join us with this fun and interactive lesson of Cantonese led by CCSF's Cantonese professsor Grace Yu. You will learn basic conversational cantonese phrases and words used in everyday life. Location will be at the Great Star Theater.

Day Three: Sunday, May 18

Community Street Fair

Kicking off the last day of the festival is a community street fair along Grant Avenue from California to Broadway Street. Have a walkthrough of different booths introducing you to different topics about cantonese history, food, landmarks, culture, and more.

Calligraphy

After the streefair have a small lesson on Chinese calligraphy.

Closing Ceremony

Closing off our festival is a closing parade of lion dance and dragon dance meeting back on the main stage on Grant Avenue.
